A common failure mode in long-running RL is that the network gradually loses its ability
to adapt — gradients shrink, features ossify, performance plateaus even when more data
arrives. This is **plasticity loss**.

My recent work tackles plasticity from two angles:

1. **Architectural** — *Plasticity-Aware Mixture of Experts* (PA-MoE) routes traffic
   between experts so that the system as a whole stays adaptive even when individual
   experts saturate. We provide theoretical justification and validate on adaptive
   video streaming under non-stationary QoE.

2. **Diagnostic** — companion preprints characterize the conditions under which
   plasticity collapses, and propose remedies that can be folded into existing
   pipelines.

The long-term goal is to produce **continually learning agents** that don't need to be
restarted from scratch every time the environment shifts.
