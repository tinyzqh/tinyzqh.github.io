Real coordination problems rarely fit neatly into the single-agent frame. My work in
multi-agent RL focuses on **action-space design** and **scalability** — making sure
that as the agent count grows, both training and inference remain feasible.

- *Scalable and Reliable MARL for Traffic Assignment* (*Communications in Transportation
  Research* 2025, IF 14.5) targets city-scale traffic networks, with an action-space
  formulation that handles fleets of agents without combinatorial blow-up.

- During my time at *InspirAI* (2022–2023), I built a general-purpose card-game AI
  framework deployed across 三国杀, 炉石, 斗地主, and 掼蛋 — the GuanDan deployment
  delivered a **+6% win rate** against the previous baseline.

- At *Baidu* (2021), I proposed and shipped *EDA-MAPPO* (Expert-Data-Assisted MAPPO),
  pushing a multi-agent policy directly into a client production environment.

The throughline: multi-agent RL only matters if it survives contact with deployment.
